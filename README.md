# Dano-airline
Dano airline passengers satisfaction rate analysis
## About Project
For this challenge, you'll be assuming the role of a Data Analyst for Dano Airlines, a Uk-based airline 
headquartered in London, United Kingdom. The latest passenger survey results just came in and it looks 
like the satisfaction rate dipped under 50% for the first time ever. The leadership team needs to take 
action fast, so they've brought you in to analyze the data and find the key areas to focus on for getting 
back on track. Your task is to recommend a data-driven strategy for increasing Dano Airlines' satisfaction rate, 
and present it in the form of a single page report or dashboard.

## Analysis and Recommendations
### Insight:
 Satisfaction has dropped below 50% — but it's not happening evenly across the airline. Economy passengers are the one majorly unsatisfied: only about 1 in 5 are satisfied, and they make up roughly half of everyone we surveyed. Even the loyal, repeat customers aren't far behind, across all class, under half of them are happy, which is a cause for concern, since these are the people who've already given us multiple chances.
### Main problem
Two things stand out as the biggest reasons people are unhappy: online boarding and Wi-Fi. The interesting part is how they affect satisfaction, a slightly-better-than-bad rating doesn't really help. People who rate either of these a 1, 2, or 3 are almost equally unhappy. It's only once the rating crosses into "good" (a 4 or 5) that satisfaction jumps dramatically. In other words, small tweaks won't make much difference, we need to get these experiences genuinely good, not just less bad. On the flip side, things like gate location and flight timing barely affect satisfaction at all, so they're not worth prioritizing right now.
### Recommendations:
 Focus the next round of investment on fixing online boarding and in-flight Wi-Fi for Economy passengers specifically, and aim high enough to cross that "good" threshold, not just nudge the numbers slightly. At the same time, we should build something specifically for our returning customers, since loyalty alone clearly isn't translating into a better experience for them today.
